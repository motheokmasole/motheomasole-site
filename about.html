Where it started

By most measures I was doing fine. Studying mechanical engineering at the University of Bath, hitting the marks, on the track everyone said was the right one. But somewhere in there I noticed how much of my life I’d never actually chosen — the routine, the goals, the version of “success” I was chasing. It had all just been handed to me, and I’d taken it.

[ Add a specific moment here — the term, the conversation, the low week, the book — that made you stop and question the autopilot. One real, concrete story does more than a page of theory. ]

The opposite of drifting isn’t hustle. It’s intention.

What changed

I started treating my own life a bit like an engineering problem: notice what’s actually happening, question the defaults, run small experiments, keep what works. Not to optimise myself into a machine — the opposite. To make sure the life I was building was one I’d actually choose again.

Why the show and the letter

Because most people my age aren’t short on ambition — they’re short on access to the right conversations and the right kind of honesty. I wanted to help close that gap.

With Intention is the podcast — long conversations about purpose, identity, and ambition, with people who’ve found real clarity, plus honest solo episodes of my own. WILIOM Wednesdays is the one-minute letter — a public journal, one honest reflection at a time.

If you’re somewhere between eighteen and twenty-five and you feel that quiet pull to do this decade properly — you’re exactly who I’m making this for.
